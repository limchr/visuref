About visart.app

Hey there, welcome to visart.app! Great to have you here. This app has one simple mission:

Revealing the essential information hidden in reference images for drawing, painting, and sketching

Why visart.app?

Here's the thing about creating art from references ‚Äì our brains are constantly playing tricks on us:

  • We see what we think is there, not what's actually there
  • Our minds automatically filter out shadows and tonal values
  • Complex forms get oversimplified ‚Äì a face becomes a circle with two dots and a curved line
  • We miss crucial measurements and proportions between elements

This app offers simple, intuitive tools to analyze what's really in your reference images. Discover true tonal values, measure proportions accurately, understand composition, and see colors as they actually are ‚Äì not as your brain interprets them.

How It Works

Simply select an image from our reference gallery or upload your own for analysis. No login required, no subscription needed ‚Äì just pure, focused tools designed to help you see like an artist.

About the reference gallery: All images in the 'Simple Shapes' category were rendered by me using Blender. The 'Still Life' photos were captured with professional lighting (softbox + DSLR). These images are public domain ‚Äì free to use in your projects! Though if you do use them, a shoutout to visart.app would be appreciated.

Key Features

  • Convert images to grayscale to study tonal values
  • Adjust brightness and contrast to analyze composition and lighting
  • Prepare reference materials for traditional or digital art
  • Understand color relationships and value structures
  • Create study materials for art classes or workshops

Whether you're a beginner learning the fundamentals or an experienced artist refining your technique, visart.app provides the essential tools to enhance your reference image workflow ‚Äì without the complexity of professional software.

About the Creator

I'm Christian Limberg, a computer scientist from Berlin, Germany. By day, I work as a postdoc researcher in AI. By night (and weekends), I'm always cooking up creative projects ‚Äì from 3D printing to music to art tools.

visart.app was born from my own frustration during drawing studies. Elements were never the size I thought they were, and values were always surprisingly lighter or darker than expected. So I applied some computer vision techniques from my day job to solve these problems, and this little app emerged.

I've kept it minimal and focused because I believe good tools should enhance creativity, not complicate it.
